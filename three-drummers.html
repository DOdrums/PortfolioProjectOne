Three Drummers

To get a good feel for what big band drumming should sound like, we'll have a listen to three drummers. There are a good many jazz and big band drummers that could play a roll in the development of your style, but these three drummers personally had a big impact on how I approach big band playing. It is encouraged to listen to many different big bands and drummers, to get a broad profile of the big band drummer.

Mel Lewis

We'll start off this section with Mel Lewis, a drummer with a very particular sound. Known for his work with the 'Mel Lewis/Thad Jones Orchestra', he has an amazing track record, playing with many of the greats in jazz music.

Mel Lewis was unique in his big band playing, heaving a very relaxed sound and playing almost exclusively at softer volumes. In the book 'The View From The Back Of The Band' (a biography of Mel Lewis), he is quoted in a conversation with Stan Kenton:

"When I joined Kenton, that very first night, he came over and yelled, 'Hey Mel, can you play louder?' And I said, 'No'. He said, 'Okay, I just thought I'd ask'"
